LONDON, UNITED KINGDOM:

Market Overview:
London is one of the world's top destinations for short-term rentals, with millions of tourists, business travelers, and temporary residents seeking accommodation annually. The market is mature, competitive, and highly regulated.

Areas We Serve:
- Zone 1 (Central London): Westminster, City of London, Camden, Kensington & Chelsea
- Zone 2: Hackney, Tower Hamlets, Hammersmith & Fulham, Wandsworth, Islington
- Zone 3-6: All Greater London areas
- We manage properties across all London boroughs

Popular London Neighborhoods for Airbnb:
- Shoreditch (trendy, creative, young professionals)
- King's Cross (central, transport hub, universities)
- Notting Hill (charming, upscale, family-friendly)
- Covent Garden (tourist hotspot, theater district)
- Canary Wharf (business district, corporate travelers)
- South Bank (cultural, riverside, attractions)
- Greenwich (historic, parks, family-friendly)
- Camden (markets, music scene, eclectic)

London Market Characteristics:
- High demand year-round
- Peak seasons: Summer (June-August), Christmas/New Year
- Average nightly rates: £80-300+ depending on location and property
- Average occupancy: 70-85% with good management
- Guest mix: 40% leisure tourists, 35% business travelers, 25% other

London Regulations:
- 90-Day Rule: Primary residences can be rented up to 90 days/year without planning permission
- Commercial Properties: Different rules apply; we help navigate compliance
- Council Tax: May differ for short-term lets
- Registration Requirements: Some boroughs require registration
- Safety Requirements: Smoke alarms, carbon monoxide detectors, gas safety certificates
- EPC Rating: Energy Performance Certificate required (minimum E rating)

Keey ensures full compliance with all London regulations.

London Time Zone:
- GMT (Greenwich Mean Time) in winter
- BST (British Summer Time / GMT+1) in summer
- Important for calendar management and guest communications

Typical London Property Performance:
- Studio/1-bed: £1,500-2,500/month average income
- 2-bed: £2,500-4,000/month average income
- 3-bed: £3,500-6,000/month average income
- 4+ bed: £5,000-10,000+/month average income

(Varies significantly by location, quality, and seasonality)

DUBAI, UNITED ARAB EMIRATES:

Market Overview:
Dubai is an rapidly growing market for short-term rentals, popular with luxury tourists, business travelers, and expats. The market is newer, expanding quickly, and presents excellent opportunities.

Areas We Serve:
- Dubai Marina (waterfront, luxury, expats)
- Downtown Dubai (Burj Khalifa area, high-end, tourists)
- Jumeirah Beach Residence (JBR) (beachfront, resorts, families)
- Palm Jumeirah (iconic, luxury, beachfront)
- Business Bay (business district, skyscrapers, corporate)
- Dubai Hills (residential, golf, families)
- City Walk (urban lifestyle, shopping, dining)
- Arabian Ranches (suburban, villas, families)

Popular Dubai Neighborhoods for Short-Term Rentals:
- Dubai Marina (most popular for short-term rentals)
- JBR (beach proximity, resorts, entertainment)
- Downtown (luxury, attractions, business)
- Palm Jumeirah (exclusive, high-end market)
- Business Bay (business travelers, central location)

Dubai Market Characteristics:
- Rapidly growing short-term rental market
- Peak seasons: November-March (winter), Ramadan varies
- Summer (June-August) is lower demand due to extreme heat
- Average nightly rates: Higher than London (AED 400-2,000+)
- Guest mix: 50% leisure tourists, 30% business, 20% long-term (1-3 months)
- Luxury market emphasis

Dubai Regulations:
- DTCM License Required: Dubai Tourism & Commerce Marketing license for short-term rental
- Registration: All properties must be registered
- Classification: Properties are classified (Standard, Superior, Deluxe, etc.)
- Tourism Dirham Fee: 7-20 AED per night per bedroom (collected from guests)
- Ejari Registration: Rental contract must be registered
- Community Rules: Some communities prohibit short-term rentals; we verify before onboarding
- Visa/Business License: Required for operation

Keey handles all licensing and compliance in Dubai.

Dubai Time Zone:
- GST (Gulf Standard Time / UTC+4)
- No daylight saving time changes
- 3-4 hours ahead of London depending on season

Typical Dubai Property Performance:
- Studio: AED 4,000-7,000/month (£900-1,600)
- 1-bed: AED 6,000-10,000/month (£1,350-2,250)
- 2-bed: AED 8,000-15,000/month (£1,800-3,400)
- 3-bed: AED 12,000-25,000/month (£2,700-5,600)
- Luxury properties: Significantly higher

(1 GBP ≈ 4.5 AED approximately)
